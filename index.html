  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 3-inch piece ginger, peeled & grated (or minced)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1/2 lb sirloin tip steak, cut (against grain) into 1/8 inch thick strips
  • 1 head broccoli, chopped into bite size pieces
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp corn starch
  • rice
  1. Combine garlic, ginger, oil, soy sauce, and vinegar in ziploc. Add meat and toss to coat. Marinate for 30 minutes.
  2. After done marinating, cook broccoli until tender crisp then blanch w/ cold water.
  3. Begin the rice cooking process.
  4. Heat a skillet to medium high. Add beef and any marinade remaining in the bag. Saute 5-7 minutes, until beef is cooked through.
  5. Add oyster sauce, corn stach, and 1/2 c of water to a small bowl, stir until fully combined. Pour over the beef, allowing to come to a simmer and thicken.
  6. Add broccoli, toss to coat. Ensure it is warm.

Dough:

  • 450 g flour
  • 80 lard (can do less)
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 250 mL / g water
  • 5 g kosher salt
  1. Add flour, lard, baking powder to bowl. Use hands to pinch the lard through fingers to mix with the flour until a pebbly consistency is reached.
  2. Heat up water in microwave. Dissolve salt into water, then gradually add to flour mixture, stirring as you go.
  3. Once a dough ball forms, knead for about 5 mins. Dough should end up fairly smooth, not sticky.
  4. Divide dough into 60g portions. Roll into balls, then cover and let sit for 15 mins at room temperature.

Cooking:

  1. Preheat cast-iron skillet. Heat level will vary depending on stove power and how quickly you go.
  2. Roll out each ball as thin as you can make it (or use a tortilla press).
  3. One at a time, cook the tortillas. Time / side varies, wait until there are bubbles and browning on both sides.

Cory's Recipe. Requires some planning ahead. Intermittent hands on involvement for ~1 hr @ beginning. Then 9 hours incubation. So need to start ~10-11 hrs before you want it done.
  • Gallon milk
  • ~2 T starter cultures (Fage 5% seemed to work well)
  1. Sterilize Instant Pot. Fill w/ ~1/2" of water, 10 mins high pressure. Quick release. Empty water.
  2. Saute milk on medium for ~23 minutes (until reaches 185-190 F), cover with aluminum foil. Stir occasionally to prevent burning
  3. Let sit for ~15 mins
  4. Cool milk to 115 F. Use tap cold water bath & very large bowl. Takes about 20 mins. I like to rotate the water halfway.
  5. Whisk in the cultures. Probably smart to use a bit extra if from store bought 'gurt, they're not as alive.
  6. Use yogurt setting, cook for 9 hrs
  7. Remove to fridge. Resist temptation to stir. Let sit 12 hrs
  8. Using cheesecloth, strainer, & large bowl, strain for up to 24 hrs until yougurt reaches desired consistency
